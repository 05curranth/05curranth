Sesame, Carrot and Chargrilled Broccoli Salad


If I’m going to eat healthily during the day, I have to have something yummy ready, otherwise I am SO tempted to go for totally useless and unhelpful food like processed biscuits (somehow I can magically forget how bad they make me feel!).

This salad is brightening up the rain today, and has kept really well in the fridge for 4 days now. As my family would agree, eating so many vegetables at the same time is a big step for me! Vegetables were such a no go before, and broccoli was at the top of the ‘I WILL ABSOLUTELY NOT EAT THAT’ list!




I have always liked salad, so my solution so far has been to eat the stuff I don’t like in a way I do. There is still no way I could face boiled broccoli, but throwing some in a dry, hot pan and slightly burning it makes it completely delicious. I used tenderstem broccoli here and I don’t waste any of it. Just chop up the entire stem and throw it in with the rest of the ingredients. I also find you don’t need a dressing because of the juices from the carrot, but next time I will definitely try it with an added bit of lime juice as I reckon that would taste zingy and delicious!

Natalie xx


  • 2 bags rocket
  • 220g tenderstem broccolli
  • 1 red onion
  • 6/7 carrots
  • sunflower oil
  • sesame oil
  • sesame seeds

Method:

  • Heat a frying pan on the highest setting with NO oil and chargrill the broccoli in two batches, turning when one side starts to become blackened. Leave to cool and then chop up including the stems.
  • Heat some sunflower oil in a large pan. Chop up the carrots into matchsticks, and throw into the pan with 100ml water and about 2 tbsp sesame oil.
  • Cook until the carrots begin to soften, stir in sesame seeds to your liking and leave to cool.
  • In your serving bowl mix together the rocket, diced red onion, cubed feta, carrots and broccoli.

Happy salad eating! x
