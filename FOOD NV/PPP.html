Perfect Pear Porridge



Porridge has always been a go to breakfast for me. The problem was however, that I would pile a huge bowl full of oats and milk and then pour in a good spoon of golden syrup to sweeten it-delicious but never left me feeling that great!

As I have said in the ABOUT section, this blog is very much the start of my journey in discovering what I should and shouldnâ€™t eat so whilst I am strictly refined sugar free, other things like dairy, gluten and meat may leave or come in further down the line. I haven’t cut out dairy, but I have definitely reduced the amount that I take in, and I feel a lot better having done so.

Porridge with milk whilst filling me up, would make me bloated and uncomfortable, which is not really the ’attacking the day’ type of start I want! When I took out the milk, and used water I felt better but hated the taste.








This recipe idea came after I boiled some fruit one morning for another recipe, and had left over water which tasted so sweet and delicious. I threw in the oats and jumped around the kitchen eating my yummy porridge!

An important thing to remember is to allow the oats to sit in the water for a couple of minutes before you tuck in. It always looks like they won’t soak up the water, and it’s easy to be tempted to throw in more-creating a giant sized portion. Whilst looking like it will fill you up, I find I am even more sluggish on such a huge portion and it really is unnecessary.

So porridge rules:

  1. Boil the fruit until soft-use whatever fruit you want.
  2. Stick to 1/2 cup of oats- it’s definitely enough!

Natalie xx


  • 1 pear (or any fruit, I have used apples and plums before)
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • boiling water
    • 1
  • /2 cup oats

Method:

  • Cut up the fruit into cm cubes, keep the skin on for extra fibre.
  • Put the fruit and cinnamon in a pan with enough boiling water to allow the fruit to float in the pan
  • Boil the fruit until soft
  • Take the pan off the heat, add the oats and allow to stand for 2 minutes to soak up the liquid.
  • Eat and go attack the day!
