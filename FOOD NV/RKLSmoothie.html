Raspberry, Kale and Lime Smoothie


I HAVE BEATEN KALE.

Ok so maybe that is an exaggeration, but it feels true enough to me.

I have continually read over the last few months about the endless benefits of Kale, how you can juice it, bake it into crisps, mix it into your stews, or eat it raw, but I HATE IT. I have tried, I really have, but I just cannot eat it without being overwhelmed with the feeling that I would rather jump into a pit of snakes than swallow it. Ok that definitely is an exaggeration, but you know what I mean.

But I have once and for all found a way to tolerate this disgusting plant, and in the process help my body out in all manner of ways.






Lime is delicious in a smoothie and so easy to add, and that is what masks the kale flavour, so although I would say you could change out the fruit for whatever you prefer or you can get your hands on. The lime must stay!

I use frozen fruit because it is so expensive and rather difficult to get hold of these fruits fresh in January in the UK, but fresh also works. However the frozen fruit makes it into a sorbet which makes everything more exciting. Leave it in the fridge for a more drinkable smoothie!

Natalie xx


Serves 2

  • Handful of each of your favourite fruits. I used frozen mango, raspberries and blueberries.
  • 1/2 cup milk (almond, rice, cows etc!)
  • 4 tbsp yoghurt
  • juice 2 limes
  • handful of kale (stalks removed if necessary) Handful of each of your favourite fruits. I used frozen mango, raspberries and blueberries.

Method:

  • Throw everything in the blender and blend. Yep it’s really that simple!
