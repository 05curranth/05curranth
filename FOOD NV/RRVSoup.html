Red Roasted Vegetable Soup



Soup saves me on days when I have minimal time and need to throw vitamins down quickly. I make a big batch when I have an hour free, and then freeze portions in whatever plastic boxes I can find in the cupboards, ready for that 20 minute break I have at lunch. I’ll just pop the box in the microwave, run around picking up books, folders and paper, and then sit down for a delicious, warming bowl of goodness!

I absolutely love red soups, anything that colour looks so vibrant and scrummy to me! The taste of the roasted veg and the sage gives this one a really deep flavour.



But the star ingredient here is the balsamic vinegar. I have seen it so many times in tomato sauces for pasta, so decided to throw some in, and I promise it makes all the difference.

I use a hand blender to whizz up my soup, it’s an item which is an absolute staple in my kitchen, but if you have a bigger freestanding blender you can use that. Just make sure you let the soup cool a little first!

Natalie xx


  • 1 onion
  • 2 garlic cloves
  • 3 carrots
  • 1 bag of mixed peppers (about 7 peppers of different colours)
  • sunflower oil
  • 2 tsp dried sage
  • 6 tomatoes
  • 1 vegetable stock cube
  • A good glug of balsamic vinegar!
  • salt and pepper

Method:

  • Chop up the onion, peppers and carrots and put in a large roasting tin with the garlic cloves. Sprinkle over the sage, a good amount of sunflower oil, salt and pepper, and mix about until everything is covered.
  • Roast for about 1 and 1/2 to 2 hours (mixing them about every 30 minutes to ensure everything is covered in liquid) at 150°c, although this depends on your oven. Just make sure the vegetables are softened and juicy.
  • Heat up a large pot and put your roasted vegetables in with your chopped up tomatoes. Make sure everything is heated through.
  • Add the stock cube and fill up with boiling water until the liquid is almost covering the vegetables.
  • Bring to the boil and then lower the heat and simmer for 20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  • Blend your soup using a hand blender or allow to cool a little before putting it into a free standing one.
  • Add the balsamic vinegar and pepper to taste. YUMMY!
