Carrot Cake Muffins



This week has been a pretty stressful one! Too many tests and essays, topped off with bad news coming from every direction left me a tad stressed and tense! At many points I found myself reaching for some relief and as usual, that solace came in the kitchen.


I am so excited to share this carrot cake recipe. I have had many disasters with creating cakes without any sugar, because sugar is pretty integral to baking. Things have come out of the oven flat, burnt, sunken, bland, or with the texture of brick! So these muffins in all of their, flavoursome, cakey, yummy goodness are making me very happy. With winter quickly approaching us, the nutmeg, cinnamon and ginger are spicy and warming. My flatmate Courtney, didn’t like the cream cheese frosting, but I thought it was a great, fresh contrast to the muffin. Choose what suits you best and ENJOY!!

Natalie xx


Makes 14

For the muffins:

  • 300g carrots, peeled and chopped in small pieces
  • 1/2 cup + 3 tbsp sultanas
  • 2 cups ground almonds
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp bicarbonate soda
  • 2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp nutmeg
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 4 tbsp coconut oil, melted
  • 2 eggs
  • 2 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 cup pecans, chopped

Frosting:

  • 1 pear, peeled, cored and finely chopped
  • 200g cream cheese
  • 1/2 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup pistachios, chopped

Method:

  • Preheat the oven to 180°c
  • Put the chopped carrots and 3 tbsp sultanas in bowl with 8 tbsp water and cook for about 5 minutes in a microwave until the carrots are soft. Throw the carrots and sultanas into a blender or use a hand blender and blend until the mixture has a spreadable texture.
  • Mix the ground almonds, baking powder, bicarb, and the spices in a bowl.
  • Add the wet ingredients and the carrots and mix until all the mixture is incorporated.
  • Add the pecans and 1/2 cup sultana and mix.
  • Spoon the mixture into 14 muffin cases and bake for about 20 minutes, until a knife comes out clean.
  • To make the FROSTING, put the chopped pear in a mug and add water to cover half the pears. Put in the microwave for 2 minutes, or until the pear is really soft.
  • Allow the pears to cool a little and then mash them up. In a clean bowl, add about 150g cream cheese, 3/4 of the pear mixture and the cinnamon. Beat with a fork and then try the mixture. If you need more sweetness add more pear and if it needs to be thicker add more cheese. This part is totally up to you!!
  • When the muffins are cool enough, top with the frosting and chopped pistachios, and shock your friends that these are full of goodness!
