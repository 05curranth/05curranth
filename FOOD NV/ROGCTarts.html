Red Onion and Goats Cheese Tartlets



Every Tuesday in our house, between the months of September and November we pile people into the living room and enjoy an hour of high drama in The Great British Bake Off.

This always involves some form of baked good, last week cinnamon rolls, the week before chocolate cupcakes, but in my real effort to keep the sugar intake to as close to 0 as possible, I have decided to make savoury treats for us to devour instead.



Day-dreaming in my economics lecture this week was very much centred on little tartlets and some leftover thyme in the fridge, and eventually I came up with these gems.

I have to say they went down very well! I made the pastry in the morning, left it in the fridge all day and then rolled it, baked it and added the filling which was all so straightforward.

Natalie xx


Pastry makes: 24-30 depending on the size of your tin and the thickness of the pastry, Topping makes: 12

  • 250g plain flour
  • 100g cold, unsalted butter
  • 50g grated parmesan
  • pinch salt
  • egg yolk
  • 1 red onion
  • sunflower oil
  • 4 tbsp balsamic vinegar
  • bunch thyme
  • 1 garlic clove
  • goats cheese or boursin

Method:

  • Sieve the flour into a mixing bowl with the salt and parmesan.
  • Add the cubed butter and rub until it looks like breadcrumbs
  • Add the egg yolk and about 3-4 tbsp’s of cold water and mix until a dough comes together.
  • Wrap the ball of dough in clingfilm and pop in the fridge for at least an hour or so (I left mine in all day)
  • Butter/oil some muffin/bun tins (I used shallow ones, but if you want bigger tarts then choose bigger sizes)
  • Roll out the pastry on a floured surface until 1/2cm thick, and cut out rounds to fill your tin. Gently lower the pastry in, and push the edges into the corners. Prick the bottom with a fork so that the pastry doesn’t puff up.
  • Bake at 180°c for 10-12 minutes.
  • Meanwhile, chop up the onion really finely and add to a pan on a low heat to gently caramelise with a splash of sunflower oil, the balsamic vinegar and about 1 tbsp of thyme leaves.
  • Remove the pastry once the tartlets begins to develop a golden colour. Spoon in the onion mixture, sprinkle on the chopped garlic, and add either thin slices of goats cheese, or a teaspoon of whatever cheese you like. (I used Boursin in some). Sprinkle over some more thyme leaves and some pepper.
  • Pop them back in (ideally under a grill but I don’t have one!)for about 5 mins or until the cheese starts to melt.
  • You can eat these hot or prepare ahead and eat the cold. But if you can resist eating them straight out of the oven you are an impressive human being!
