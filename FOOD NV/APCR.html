Apple, Pecan and Cinnamon Rolls

When you mention the fact that you are eating a sugar free diet people look at you with a slight horror in their eyes. ‘How can you possibly live without cake or chocolate or biscuits?’.

It’s taking time to figure out these recipes, but using fruit, or agave syrup or other ‘outside the box’ ideas it is actually possible to eat some cake and not feel so horrible after.

It is worth noting that different people will say different things about the advantages and disadvantages of certain sugar substitutues. I’m not keen to use anything that pretends to be sugar, but really is a whole load of chemicals, because that isn’t going to be any better. Some people are a big fan of Agave Syrup, it has a low glychemic index meaning it will not spike your sugar levels so much. Others say that it is no better than eating normal sugar.






Personally I use it sparingly, but I definitely notice that I feel a lot better after eating something with agave compared to eating something with normal sugar.

A couple of my friends just look confused at the idea of taking a cake and not putting in sugar, so I know I have got it right when they like what I have given them! This was the Great British Bake Off treat this week in our flat, and with the apples and raisins added they really did go down very well!

Natalie xx


For the dough:

  • 2 cups flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 2 tsp cinnamon
  • pinch salt
  • 2/3 cup soy yoghurt
  • 2 tbsp coconut oil, melted
  • 4 tsp agave syrup
  • 1/2 cup raisins

For the Filling:

  • 1 cup pecans, finely chopped
  • cinnamon
  • agave syrup
  • 2 apples, finely chopped

Method:

  • First make the dough: Sieve dry ingredients into a large bowl
  • Add the rest of the ingredients and mix until it comes together and forms a dough.
  • Roll out onto a floured surface into a large rectangle about 1 cm thick
  • Sprinkle on the pecans, apples, cinnamon and drizzle of agave syrup.
  • Roll up the dough along the longest edge to form a cylinder, and slice into 10 pieces.
  • Butter a muffin tin, and place the rolls into the tin.
  • Bake at 180°c for 10 to 12 minutes
