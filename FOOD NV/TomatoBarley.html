Tomato Paprika Pearl Barley

So it’s really cold, you’ve been outside all day, and you want to eat something warming and filling, but crucially something which won’t leave you in bed all afternoon in a food coma!

Combining a bundle of vitamins, a low GI carb and yummy flavours, as well as being simple to make, this tomato pearl barley is perfect. The spices in the sauce really make the difference in this recipe, because we all know tomato sauces can be a bit - well ‘samey’ right?

If you don’t have these exact spices then experiment with a different herb- thyme would work really well. If you don’t have any spices in your cupboard, it is well worth investing in a few and having a play around, plus they keep forever!




It can be easy to pile this onto your plate and slowly fall into the yummiest food coma you have ever experienced, but then your day is pretty much over, and I find that my sensitive digestive system really isn’t happy with over eating.

A great way to avoid this is to put half your normal portion size onto your plate, and eat slowly. Put your cutlery down between mouthfuls, don’t continually shovel. Talk to your friends or read a magazine- savour your food! This way you will feel full earlier and avoid the sluggish food coma situation.

Oh and this will serve 3 people comfortably, but if you have more, just add more pearl barley.

Natalie xx


Serves 3

  • 1 carrot, peeled and slice
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 1 garlic clove, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • pinch cumin
  • 4 spring onions, sliced
  • 1/2 red pepper, cubed
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tin tomatoes
  • 75g pearl barley, depending on how hungry you are and how many people you are feeding
  • 7 cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 12 olives
  • feta cheese

Method:

  • Boil the carrot in water for about 10 minutes. Once they are boiled save the water in a bowl for later
  • Fry the garlic and onion in a deep, non-stick pan with the rosemary and cumin until soft.
  • Add the spring onions, pepper, carrot and paprika and fry for 5 minutes.
  • Add the tomatoes and bring to the boil, add the pearl barely.
  • Over 30 minutes add the carrot water slowly.
  • Add the cherry tomatoes and olives and boil for a further 10 minutes.
  • Serve the pearl barley and top with as much crumbled feta as you
