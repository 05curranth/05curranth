Apple, Pear and Raspberry Breakfast Muffins



Breakfast has to be one of the funnest parts of my day. Luckily I don’t have to be in class until 11AM, and so at about 7.30 in the morning I find myself in the kitchen opening the cupboards and turning on the oven. This is the time when I prepare big batches of salads and soups to have over the week, but also when I can cook some breakfast treats. Eating sugar free means you can have pudding for breakfast every day!






One of favourite recipes is this breakfast muffin. I really don’t feel that bad about eating 3 or 4 as they have no sugar, and just fruit to keep them sweet. I love the cinnamon taste, but as many people will agree with I like to add cinnamon to just about everything. Also keeping the skin on the apples and pears makes no difference to the taste once it’s all blitzed up and keeps an extra bit of fibre in.

If you’re not used to eating cake without sugar then these do seem different (my Mum says they taste ‘healthy’). But I promise the sweetness of the raspberries and the subtle background flavours make them

Natalie xx


  • 200g self-raising flour
  • 50g porridge oats , plus 1 tbsp for the top
  • ½ tsp cinammon
  • 2 apples
  • 1 pear
  • ½ tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 2 medium eggs
  • 150ml fat-free yogurt
  • 6 tbsp sunflower oil
  • 20 frozen raspberries or other berries.

Method:

  • Heat the oven to 180°C
  • Mix the flour and oats in a bowl with the cinnamon.
  • Core the apples and pear and put in a blender with the skins on until they form a grated texture.
  • Stir in the apple and pear mixture, bicarb, eggs, yogurt and oil and mix everything together.
  • Line a muffin tray with 10 paper cases and half fill them with the mixture, then dot 2 raspberries on top of each and cover with the rest of the mixture. Sprinkle the extra oats over the top of the muffins. Bake for 25 minutes or until golden, risen and cooked through.
