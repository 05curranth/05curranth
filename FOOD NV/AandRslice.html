Apple and Raisin Slice



It has been SO COLD the last few days and the fact that the it’s dark whilst I am still working in the library doesn’t mean I am pulling all nighters…it means that the nights are drawing in and the delicious spicy flavours of winter are valid to throw into your cooking.

My flatmates will agree that I need no excuse to put cinnamon into any recipe, but now I have a reason to add it to everything and people can muse about winter flavours!



The first time I made this I threw a great slop of raisins on the top, which really was too sweet-this balance is definitely a lot better! It might look a bit messy, but that’s because you can put it together so quickly, you really aren’t too bothered about what it looks like. And honestly when everyone is digging in, it is gone so quickly that it’s not too much of a problem!

Natalie xx


For the base:

  • 32 cups oats
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 2 tsp cinnamon
  • 4 tsp coconut oil, melted
  • 2 tbsp soy yoghurt
  • 1/2 cup pecans
  • 1 apple, finely chopped

For the Topping:

  • 1/2 cup raisins
  • 1 apple sliced

Method:

  • Preheat the oven at 180°c. Line a 20cmx20cm square tin with greasproofpaper
  • Mix the dry ingredients in a large bowl
  • Add the rest of the ingredients and mix into a thick paste
  • Spoon into the tin and press down firmly with a fork
  • Bake in the oven for 15 minutes.
  • Meanwhile heat the raisins in a pan with half a cup of boiling water
  • Remove the base from the oven and spread the raisins evenly it, keeping a little of the water in the pan. Top with the sliced apple and sprinkle some cinnamon over the apples followed by the remaining water, and put back in the oven for another 10 minutes.
  • Eat and feel autumnal!
