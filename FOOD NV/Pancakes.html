Pancakes with Raspberry and Blueberry Sauce

For some reason Halloween means doughnuts to the supermarkets. Every time I go to get some shopping I am immediately faced with those scrummy looking, health damaging rings covered in chocolate icing and orange sprinkles. Resisting things like processed looking biscuits and chocolate bars is getting easier all the time, but just the sight of those doughnuts gets my tastebuds going and my mind thinking ‘oh just 1 is ok right?’. The trouble with me however, is that one means 10, and 10 means 30 and then I might as well have some tomorrow right?!! NOT GOOD!!! So on my way back from the library the other night I found the only way to not take a diversion by the supermarket was to envision some yummy pancakes in the warm kitchen with the girls.




IThis recipe is, of course, sugar free and I am finding that the best way to make sugar free bakes that don’t taste like paper is by adding spices and plenty of other flavours. Cinnamon, as I’m sure you will notice, is 1 of my go to spices, and the fruit adds that sweetness that you are craving. If you want to make them dairy free then switch the milk for something lactose free (I use unsweetened almond milk), and some melted coconut oil. You might need a little more liquid because almond milk is thicker. Good for breakfast, lunch or late night snacking in the kitchen!!

Natalie xx


Makes enough for 3-4 people

  • 1/4 cup raspberries
  • 1/4 cup blueberries (you can use frozen fruit if you want to)
  • 1 cup porridge oats
  • 1/2 cup plain flour
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2-1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 egg
  • 3/4 cup milk / unsweetened almond milk
  • 2 tbsp butter / coconut oil, melted

Method:

  • Put the fruit into a saucepan, add 1/2 cup of boiling water and bring up to the boil. Reduce the heat and allow to simmer, stirring regularly, as you make the pancakes.
  • Mix the dry ingredients in a bowl. Add the wet ingredients and mix until everything is combined.
  • Heat a nonstick saucepan (you don’t need to add any butter or oil because you have that in the batter)
  • Spoon the mixture into the pan to make pancakes about 5cm in diameter. Flip them over once they begin to brown on the bottom.
  • Serve with your sauce, and a little more cinnamon if you want!
