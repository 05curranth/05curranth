Date and Apricot Bread



"Acorns were good until bread was found."

I think this quote applies to a vast array of foods which in comparison to a beautifully baked loaf of fresh bread straight from the oven, shrink a long way into the background. Nothing gets my flatmates out of bed quite like the smell of dough cooking in the oven, and this bread was definitely no exception!


It was also a case of using whatever ingredients I had in the kitchen cupboard. I actually set out to make a different loaf, and then found a rather measly option of ingredients and a very strong desire to not change out of my pyjamas to go to the supermarket, so a can of apricots and chopped up dates it was to be!

I&rsquot;m glad of this though, as this combination is just delicious, and kept me filed up throughout the next few afternoons of the week wen I was craving a sweet treat (sugar free of course!)

Have a lovely week

Natalie x


Makes 1 good sized loaf…easily eatable in 1 sitting with good friends!!

  • 100g porridge oats
  • 25g butter/coconut oil (melted)
  • 400g plain flour
  • 30g Chia seeds
  • 2 tbsp agave syrup
  • 1 tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1 1/2 tsp mixed spice
  • 2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 1/2 tsp ground ginger
  • 50g dates (chopped)
  • 1 can apricots in juice, or about 10 fresh apricots (chopped) 450 ml buttermilk

Method:

  • Preheat the oven to 200°C
  • Rub the butter into the oats or stir in the coconut oil.
  • Stir in the sieved flour, agave syrup, bicarb, spices and fruit.
  • Slowly add the buttermilk, you may not need it all so go carefully!
  • Bring the mixture into a ball, and place on a floured baking sheet.
  • Bake for about 30-35 minutes. The dough needs to be really firm, but it will need to stand for a little while to let it all set. Using buttermilk makes a denser and differently structured bread.
