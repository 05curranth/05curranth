Hot Chocolate

Nobody’s perfect right?! I read a lot about this in Kris Carr’s: Crazy Sexy Diet which I recommend to anyone thinking about changing their diet or lifestyle. New Year contained a few eating blips, however looking back on it, I still probably ate less than a quarter of the amount of refined sugar that I usually do during the holidays, so instead of seeing it as a failure I am very much counting that as an achievement. In the last few months I have made enormous changes, and even more valuably I have made some real discoveries about what motivates me.



The changes I want to see will take months, maybe years and that’s ok, but to keep me motivated I need to feel a sense of improvement or success more regularly. Rewards are also a huge part of this, but instead of turning to refined sugar and TV, instead I will buy some new sports clothing once reaching a goal or treat myself with some fruit or…HOT CHOCOLATE.

Pretty much everyday since New Year I have had a mug of hot chocolate whilst knowing that it is doing wonders for my body.

Cacoa is the unrefined version of cocoa, and so contains bundles of anti-oxidants, vitamins, and minerals AND actually raises the level of serotinin which improves your mood. So it scientifically makes you feel better. Works for me!! Check out Deliciously Ella’s page for even more factual goodness.

The first few attempts at making this didn’t go quite to plan, so I selflessly continued to test until I found the perfect method!

You are welcome!

Natalie x


Serves 1 person

  • 1 square of cacoa (my block came from Marks & Spencers)
  • 1 mug of milk (cow’s/almond/rice, just make sure there isn’t any added sugar)
  • Spices of your choice, I love cinnamon, but nutmeg and ginger work too.
  • If you need sweetness add a little pure maple syrup or agave syrup.

Method:

  • Melt the square of chocolate in a mug in the microwave on a low heat.
  • In another mug heat the milk.
  • Slowly pour the milk into the melted cacoa stirring continuously. If it goes a bit lumpy, put it back in the microwave…just watch for unwanted bubbling over!
  • Sprinkle a little spice and syrup if you need it, and stir to make the most delicious and healthy treat!!
